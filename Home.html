The Importance of Inclusivity

Inclusivity is a fundamental right for deaf people, and it is crucial to ensure that they are not excluded from society. Inclusive practices create an environment where deaf individuals can thrive and achieve their full potential without facing discrimination or barriers. Inclusive education provides access to qualified teachers, inclusive curriculum, and appropriate accommodations to meet their needs, ensuring equal opportunities for learning. Inclusive employment practices provide access to job postings, interviews, and workplace accommodations, allowing deaf individuals to work in an inclusive and safe environment. Social inclusion ensures that deaf people are not excluded from public spaces, events, and social activities. It is essential to promote inclusivity to break down social stigmas and barriers and foster a more welcoming and diverse society that values and respects the contributions of all individuals, including those who are deaf.

Web Accesibility

The ADA has set guidlines for website accessiblity. If your website does not comply to the guidlines, there are legal penalties. Why? The internet has become our main source of news, information, entertainment, and more. It is a vital part of our lives and therefore it should be accessible to all.

You can visit the ADA's Website to check your website afore accesibility errors.

Tips for Deaf and Hard of Hearing accessibility

o Provide multiple contact methods - Phone number, email, web chat, video call options

o Add subtitles and captions to videos

o Proper content structure - This is important for other accessibility features to be used with your website. A poorly structured website might not work with outside programs.

o Summarize audio and video content - decriptions will better encapsulate the purspose of your content
